Earth Overview

Earth is the third planet from the Sun, approximately **4.54 billion years old**, and the only known world with liquid water, life, and a stable atmosphere. Our home is a dynamic system of interacting spheres.

Structure of Earth

  • Crust: The relatively thin, solid outermost layer.
  • Mantle: A thick, semi-solid layer of rock where convection drives tectonic movement.
  • Outer Core: A hot, liquid layer of iron and nickel, generating Earth's magnetic field.
  • Inner Core: A dense, incredibly hot **solid** sphere of metal at the planet's center.

Atmosphere & Climate

Earth's protective blanket is composed primarily of **78% nitrogen** and **21% oxygen**.

Biosphere (Life)

The biosphere is the global sum of all ecosystems. Earth hosts millions of species, from microbes to blue whales, thriving in diverse environments like forests, oceans, deserts, and tundra. All life is interconnected through complex **food webs**.

Plate Tectonics & Geology

The lithosphere is divided into several large and small tectonic plates. Their slow, continuous movement (continental drift) is the driving force behind major geological events, including **earthquakes**, **volcanoes**, and the formation of **mountain ranges**.

Earth in Space

Earth orbits the Sun every **365.25 days** and rotates on its axis every **24 hours**. The planet's axial tilt of approximately **$23.5^{\circ}$** is the primary reason for the seasonal changes we experience.

History of Earth (Eons)

  • **Hadean Eon:** The Earth forms and its surface solidifies.
  • **Archean Eon:** The first simple life (prokaryotes) appears.
  • **Proterozoic Eon:** Oxygen levels rise significantly (Great Oxidation Event).
  • **Phanerozoic Eon:** The explosion of complex life, including fish, dinosaurs, mammals, and ultimately, humans.

Environmental Issues

Current challenges facing the planet require global action:

  • **Climate Change:** Long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns.
  • **Pollution:** Contamination of air, water, and land.
  • **Deforestation:** Clearing of the planet's essential forests.
  • **Extinction Crisis:** The accelerated loss of biodiversity.

Fun Facts

  • Earth travels around the Sun at an incredible speed of approximately **107,000 km/h** ($67,000$ mph).
  • The deepest known point in Earth's oceans is the **Mariana Trench** ($11$ km deep).
  • The largest mountain on Earth, measured from base to peak, is **Mauna Kea** in Hawaii.
