Basic Facts

Venus is the second planet from the Sun, named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty. It is a terrestrial planet, often called Earth's "sister planet" because of its similar size and bulk composition, though its surface conditions are radically different.

  • Diameter: 12,104 km ($\approx$ 95% of Earth's)
  • Average Distance from Sun: 108 million km
  • Surface Gravity: 0.904 $g$
  • Length of Day (Sidereal Period): 243 Earth days (longest rotation period in the Solar System)
  • Length of Year (Orbital Period): 225 Earth days
  • Moons: None

Surface Features & Geology

The surface of Venus is hidden beneath thick clouds but has been mapped extensively by radar, notably by the Magellan spacecraft. It is dominated by vast, smooth volcanic plains, two large highland regions (Ishtar Terra and Aphrodite Terra), and relatively few impact craters, suggesting the entire planet underwent a major resurfacing event about 300 to 600 million years ago.

The planet is covered in more than 1,000 volcanic structures and features like coronae (large, ring-like structures) and tesserae (highly deformed terrain).

Exploration Highlights

Venus was the first planet visited by a spacecraft (Mariner 2 in 1962) and the first on which a soft landing was achieved. The Soviet Venera program was particularly successful in studying its harsh environment.

  • Venera Program (USSR): Successfully landed multiple probes, providing the first (and so far, only) images from the Venusian surface.
  • Magellan (NASA): Used radar to map 98% of the surface in incredible detail during the early 1990s.
  • Akatsuki (JAXA): Currently in orbit, dedicated to studying the atmospheric dynamics, particularly the super-rotation.
  • Future Missions: NASA plans two new missions, DAVINCI+ (Decadal Survey to sample the atmosphere) and VERITAS (Venus Emissivity, Radio Science, InSAR, Topography, and Spectroscopy), scheduled for launch around 2030.

Fun Facts & Unique Characteristics

  • Retrograde Rotation: Venus rotates backward (clockwise) compared to most other planets, meaning the Sun would appear to rise in the **West** and set in the East.
  • Brightest Object: It is the second brightest natural object in the night sky after the Moon, often called the "Morning Star" or "Evening Star."
  • Day Longer Than Year: Venus takes 243 Earth days to rotate once, but only 225 Earth days to orbit the Sun once.
  • No Magnetic Field: Unlike Earth, Venus lacks a global magnetic field, leaving its atmosphere directly exposed to the solar wind.
